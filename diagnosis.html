There are many tests that exist today that can help you find out whether you or your future children may have Cystic Fibrosis. Consult with your doctor before considering any of these:
Immunoreactive Trypsinogen test
  • A newborn screening test which checks for abnormal levels of the protein IRT in the blood. High levels may signal cystic fibrosis
Sweat Chloride Test
  • It checks for increased levels of salt in the sweat. The test is performed by using a chemical that makes the skin sweat when triggered by a weak electric current. Sweat is collected on a pad or paper and then analyzed.
Septum Test
  • The doctor takes a sample of mucus. The sample can confirm the presence of a lung infection.
Chest X-Ray
  • To find blockages or buildups of mucus
CT Scan
  • using a combination of X-rays taken from many different directions your doctor can view internal structures, such as the liver and pancreas, making it easier to assess the extent of organ damage caused by cystic fibrosis.
Pulmonary function Tests
  • The tests can help measure how much air can be inhaled or exhaled and how well the lungs transport oxygen to the rest of the body. Any abnormalities in these functions may indicate cystic fibrosis.
Carrier Screening
  • CF is passed from parents to children through genes. You can get a screening test to find out if you carry the gene for CF.

Prenatal Screening
  • Chorionic villus sampling
    • This test checks tissue from the placenta to see if a baby has a genetic condition, like CF.
    • You can get CVS at 10 to 12 weeks of pregnancy.
  • Amniocentesis
    • Checks amniotic fluid from around your baby in the uterus (womb) for genetic conditions, like CF.
    • You can get this test at 15 to 20 weeks of pregnancy.
