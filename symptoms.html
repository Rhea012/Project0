Respiratory Symptoms
  • Thick, sticky mucus clogs the tube that carries air in/out of your lungs, which causes:
    • A persistent cough that produces thick mucus
    • Wheezing
    • Exercise intolerance
    • Repeated lung infections
    • Inflamed nasal passages or a stuffy nose
Digestive Symptoms
  • Thick mucus blocks tubes carrying digestive enzymes from pancreas to your small intestine. Without these digestive enzymes, intestines aren't able to completely absorb the nutrients in food you which results in:
    • Foul-smelling, greasy stools
    • Poor weight gain and growth
    • Intestinal blockage, particularly in newborns
    • Chronic or severe constipation
