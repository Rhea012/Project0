Cystic Fibrosis
What Symptoms Diagnosis Treatments
Cystic Fibrosis is a genetic disorder that causes severe damage to the lungs, digestive system and other organs in the body. Those with Cystic Fib. have cells that produce thicker secretions, clogging passages instead of making them slippery. When chloride ions cannot leave the cell properly through the Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane conductance Regulator (CFTR), water is retained in the cell due to osmosis. These fluid are thicker than they should be. This is an Autosomal Recessive disorder and children have a 25% chance of inheriting it from their parents. It is caused by a mutation in the CTFR gene, which regulates the movement of salt in an out of cells. This causes dysfuncional regulation of cell electrolytes and water. Unfortuately the effects of the disorder are widespread across daily life. Some of them include:
Physical Effects
  • As time passes, cystic fibrosis becomes more severe with more intensive symptoms and higher probability of developing related conditions.
  • Respiratory diseases are the most common and include damaged airways, chronic infections, growths in the nose, coughing blood, peumothorx, and respirtory failure
  • Thinning of the bones (osteoporosis) and electrolyte imbalances and dehydration can also occur
  • Puberty can be delayed a couple years
Lifestyle Effects
  • The lives of children with CF are very different from those with more normal lifestyles
    • Great demands regarding feeding, treatments and procedures
  • As patients become adults or in the cases when the disease is only diagnosed as adults, it is demanding to balance a normal adult life with managing a chronic disease
    • Symptoms, treatments, work, family, emotions, and social experiences to cope with.
  • Lots of responsibilities to deal with treatment
Psychological Effects
  • Majority of men with CFB are infertile and women may have difficulties conceiving which can cause depression
  • According to the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation, anxiety is one of the most common emotional issues faced by these patients
  • Increased likelihood of psychiatric problems, such as depression, and of scoring poorly on physical functioning measures of quality of life
  • Feelings of frustration from the responsibilities which come with treatment can cause anxiety and stress disorders
