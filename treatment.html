No real cure has been found for this genetic disorder. Instead scientists have found treatments to lessen CF's everyday stresses. Consult a doctor before trying any of these.
Medication
  • Antibiotics
    • To get rid of infections in the lungs, and prevent them from possibly forming
  • Mucus Thinning medications
    • make the mucus thinner and less sticky. They also help you to cough up the mucus so it leaves the lungs.
  • Bronchodilators
    • relax the muscles around the tubes that carry air to the lungs, which helps increase airflow. You can take this medication through an inhaler or a nebulizer
Surgical Procedures
  • Bowel Surgery
    • an emergency surgery that involves the removal of a section of the bowel. It may be performed to relieve a blockage in the bowels.
  • Lung Transplant
    • The surgery may be necessary when someone with cystic fibrosis has severe breathing problems. In some cases, both lungs may need to be replaced. This can potentially lead to serious complications after surgery, including pneumonia.
  • Feeding tube
    • May be surgically inserted into the stomach
Chest Physical Therapy
  • Chest Clapping
    • It is typically performed one to four times per day. A common technique involves placing the head over the edge of a bed and clapping with cupped hands along the sides of the chest.
    • Devices may be used to help with this: a chest clapper, which imitates the effects of clapping with cupped hands along the sides of the chest , or an inflatable vest, which vibrates at a high frequency to help remove chest mucus
